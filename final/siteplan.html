National Parks & Trails

WDD 231 – Individual Project Site Plan  |  Parker Jones

1. Site Name

National Parks & Trails

I chose this name because it says exactly what the site is about — the parks themselves and the trails that take you through them. It's short, easy to remember, and the ampersand ties the two ideas together in a way that feels natural rather than forced.

I have not picked a domain-style name beyond the site title because that is not required for this class project.

2. Site Purpose

National Parks & Trails is a guide to U.S. national parks built on live data from the NPS API. The idea is to give people a useful starting point for finding parks, exploring activities, and planning trips — all in one place. Specifically, it will:

  • Browse parks: Search or filter 15+ national parks by name or state, each showing a photo, description, and key details.
  • Show activities: A separate page breaks down what you can actually do at each park — hiking, ranger programs, camping, and more.
  • Save a list: Visitors can favorite parks and build a trip itinerary that stays saved in the browser via local storage.
  • Park details modal: Clicking a park card opens a modal with more info so you never lose your place on the page.

3. Scenarios

These are the kinds of questions a real visitor might ask when they land on the site:

  • "I'm planning a road trip through the Southwest. Which national parks are located in Utah and Arizona, and what beginner-friendly hiking trails do they offer?"
  • "I visited Yellowstone last summer and want to remember it. Can I save it to a favorites list or build a custom itinerary for parks I still want to see?"
  • "I have kids aged 6 and 9. Which parks offer ranger-led programs or other family activities that would be suitable for young children?"

4. Color Schema

The colors are pulled from the look of national parks — forest greens, warm sandstone, golden sunlight. All four are actively used on this page.

Deep Forest Green #1b4332 Header, nav & footer backgrounds; main section headings
Warm Cream #fef9ef Page & card backgrounds; light text on dark surfaces
Golden Amber #e9a800 Section accent borders, CTA buttons, decorative highlights
Dark Charcoal #333333 Body text, paragraph content, labels, captions

5. Typography

The site uses two Google Fonts that complement each other well. Both are loaded and active on this page.

Poppins (sans-serif) — Headings, Hero Text, Body, and Navigation

Explore America's National Parks

Discover trails, wildlife, and wonder

Poppins is used across the site for consistency. It keeps headings clear, supports readable body content, and maintains visual rhythm in navigation and buttons.

Poppins Weights in Use

Home  |  Explore  |  Plan

Weight 700 is used for prominent headings and action labels, 600 for navigation and supporting headings, and 400 for paragraph text.
